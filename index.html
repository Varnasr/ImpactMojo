ImpactMojo 101 Knowledge Series

ImpactMojo is a curated library of full-length knowledge decks that explore justice, equity, and development practice in India and South Asia. Each one is grounded in theory, applied in context, and ready for real-world use by educators, practitioners, and social researchers.

  • Decent Work for All 101
    Explores fair livelihoods, labour rights, gig work, and informality through an Indian lens, grounded in ILO’s Decent Work agenda and social protection discourse.
  • Data Feminism 101
    Challenges dominant data narratives using feminist theory. Covers intersectionality, visibility, and the politics of knowledge in South Asian development.
  • Social Safety Nets 101
    Unpacks India's welfare architecture—PDS, NREGA, pensions—through lifecycle needs and exclusion errors, with discussion on Aadhaar and last-mile barriers.
  • Climate Change 101
    Covers IPCC basics, vulnerability, adaptation, and mitigation strategies. Rooted in Indian geography and the realities of climate justice and governance.
  • Indian Constitution and Law 101
    A visual guide to India’s constitutional foundations, rights, directive principles, and the structure of its judiciary, with key legal cases and social justice themes.
  • Development Economics 101
    From growth and inequality to institutions and state capacity, this deck introduces core economic debates using Indian and South Asian policy experiences.
  • Digital Development Ethics 101
    Explores the ethics of digital tools in development, including surveillance, consent, platform governance, AI equity, and algorithmic injustice in the Global South.
  • Multivariate Analysis 101
    Introduces causal modeling, control variables, interaction terms, and statistical power for policy analysis, with real survey examples from India.
  • Bivariate Analysis 101
    Covers cross-tabs, chi-square tests, means comparisons, and correlation for social researchers working on program outcomes or demographic trends.
  • EDA for Household Survey Data 101
    Teaches univariate and distributional analysis, skewness, missingness, and data hygiene checks using real survey microdata.
  • Qualitative Research 101
    A complete guide to qualitative methods—IDIs, FGDs, ethnography, coding, consent—with Indian case examples and sectoral relevance.
  • Fundraising 101
    Covers institutional donors, HNIs, CSR trends, proposal strategies, donor reporting, and grant diversification with an Indian nonprofit context.
  • Global Development Architecture 101
    Explains how UN agencies, multilateral finance, WTO, and global partnerships shape development policy—with a decolonial critique.
  • Post-Truth Politics 101
    Examines misinformation, narrative warfare, political polarization, and trust erosion in India’s public sphere and policy discourse.
  • Data Literacy 101
    Empowers practitioners to read graphs, question indicators, spot misleading visuals, and use open datasets in everyday program work.
  • Community-Led Development 101
    Focuses on PLA tools, participatory planning, facilitation ethics, and the evolution of community-led models in India and globally.
  • Environmental Justice 101
    Explores air, water, land, and environmental decision-making through equity and procedural justice lenses in the Indian regulatory context.
  • Care Economy and Time Use 101
    Analyzes unpaid care work, time-use surveys, care infrastructure, and feminist economics, rooted in Indian labour statistics and policy debates.
  • Econometrics 101
    Covers regression, treatment effects, IVs, fixed effects, and more, using development datasets and evaluation logic with Indian examples.
  • Decolonizing Development Practice 101
    Traces colonial legacies in development thinking and offers alternative epistemologies, Southern theories, and indigenous practice frames.
  • Marginalized Identities 101
    Explores caste, tribe, gender, religion, disability, and intersectionality through rights, representation, and discrimination data in South Asia.
  • Poverty and Inequality 101
    Explains income vs multidimensional poverty, inequality metrics, redistribution, and caste/class/gender patterns using Indian datasets.
  • Political Economy 101
    Explores incentives, institutions, and power in policymaking, featuring comparative case studies and Indian governance contexts.
  • Visual Ethnography and Storytelling 101
    Introduces participatory photography, visual documentation ethics, field aesthetics, and representation frameworks for South Asian contexts.
  • Advocacy and Public Communication 101
    Connects message framing, campaign design, evidence storytelling, and political communication with real Indian movement examples.
  • Monitoring, Evaluation, and Learning (MEL) 101
    Unpacks theories of change, indicator types, evaluation tools, adaptive MEL systems, and participatory learning practices.
  • Organizational Change and Communications (OCC) 101
    Focuses on internal transformation, culture change, team learning, communications strategies, and leadership within NGOs and agencies.
  • Social Research Ethics and Consent 101
    Guides researchers through positionality, risk, harm, consent processes, and ethical dilemmas with real applied examples.
  • Women’s Economic Empowerment (WEE) 101
    Frames economic agency, control over resources, institutional barriers, and enabling ecosystems using Indian WEE program evidence.
  • Gender Studies 101
    Surveys feminist theory, gender and sexuality, patriarchy, queer movements, and legal shifts across India and South Asia.
  • Livelihoods 101
    Explores rural and urban livelihood strategies, informal sector dynamics, and sustainable economic pathways in Indian contexts.
  • Public Health 101
    Covers primary health care, OneHealth, health systems, and social determinants with South Asian equity and delivery challenges.
  • Education and Pedagogy 101
    Introduces learning theory, pedagogy, curriculum debates, and NEP 2020 themes for Indian education professionals.
More about Varna
Dr. Varna Sri Raman is a development economist, educator, and social researcher with two decades of experience across gender, climate, health, and public systems in South Asia.
She is the founder of InsightStack and the curator of OpenStacks — an open knowledge ecosystem for the development sector.
