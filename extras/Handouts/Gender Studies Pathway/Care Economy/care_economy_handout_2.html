International Best Practices

10 minutes
üá´üáÆ Finland: Universal Childcare System

Policy: Universal right to childcare from age 9 months, heavily subsidized by government

Investment: 1.4% of GDP annually

Results: 85% women's employment rate, gender pay gap reduced to 16%, improved child development outcomes

Indian Context: Would require ‚Çπ4 lakh crores annually (current total education budget: ‚Çπ1.04 lakh crores)

üá∞üá∑ South Korea: Care Infrastructure Investment

Policy: Massive infrastructure program including community care centers, after-school programs, elder care facilities

Investment: $30 billion over 5 years (2008-2013)

Results: Women's employment increased from 50% to 57%, birth rate stabilized

Indian Context: Proportional investment would be ‚Çπ15 lakh crores over 5 years

üá≤üáΩ Mexico: Estancias Infantiles Program

Policy: Childcare support for working mothers, especially in informal sector

Investment: $200 million annually

Results: 18% increase in women's labor force participation, 13% increase in their income

Indian Context: Scalable model for informal sector workers (92% of women workers)

ü§î Policy Design Questions
  1. Which international model is most applicable to India's federal structure and development priorities?
  2. How could India adapt these models to address its specific challenges (rural-urban divide, caste dynamics, informal economy)?
  3. What role should technology play in scaling care infrastructure efficiently?
  4. How can policies ensure care workers (mostly women) receive decent wages and working conditions?
  5. What indicators would you use to measure the success of care economy investments?

üéØ Workshop Synthesis

Care economy investments are economic development investments. Infrastructure, technology, and services that reduce unpaid care burdens enable women's economic participation, improve family wellbeing, and generate positive returns to government investment. The key is designing integrated approaches that recognize care work as the foundation of all economic activity.
