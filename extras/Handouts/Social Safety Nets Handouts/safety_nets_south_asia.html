What are Social Safety Nets in South Asia?

Definition: Government programs providing income support, food security, healthcare, and employment to vulnerable populations, with focus on rural poor, informal workers, women, children, and marginalized communities.

South Asian Context: Safety nets address extreme poverty, rural distress, informal economy challenges, and social exclusion while building human capital for economic growth.

South Asian Reality: Over 70% of the population works in the informal economy with limited social protection coverage

Major Indian Social Protection Programs

Employment & Livelihoods

MGNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act)

  • Coverage: 100 days guaranteed employment per rural household
  • Beneficiaries: million households annually
  • Wage rate: ₹ per day (varies by state)
  • Key features: Demand-driven, self-targeting, focuses on rural infrastructure

Impact on women: % of workers are women

PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)

  • Benefit: ₹6,000 per year to small farmer families
  • Coverage: million farmer families
  • Delivery: Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to bank accounts

Food Security

PDS (Public Distribution System)

  • Coverage: million households under National Food Security Act
  • Entitlement: 5kg grain per person per month (priority households)
  • Price: ₹2/kg rice, ₹3/kg wheat
  • Targeting: AAY (Antyodaya) and PHH (Priority Household) categories

Mid-Day Meal Scheme

  • Coverage: million children in schools
  • Objectives: Nutrition + education attendance
  • Implementation: State governments with central funding

ICDS (Integrated Child Development Services)

  • Target: Children under 6, pregnant and lactating mothers
  • Services: Nutrition, health, pre-school education
  • Delivery: Through Anganwadi centers

Healthcare

PM-JAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana)

  • Coverage: ₹5 lakh health insurance per family annually
  • Beneficiaries: million poor families
  • Model: Cashless treatment in empaneled hospitals

JSY (Janani Suraksha Yojana)

  • Focus: Institutional delivery promotion
  • Cash incentive: ₹ for rural, ₹ for urban births

Pensions & Social Security

National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP)

  • IGNOAPS: Old Age Pension - ₹ per month
  • IGNWPS: Widow Pension - ₹ per month
  • IGNDPS: Disability Pension - ₹ per month

Other South Asian Safety Net Programs

Bangladesh

  • VGD (Vulnerable Group Development): Food + training for poor women
  • Old Age Allowance: Cash transfers for elderly
  • Primary Education Stipend: Conditional cash transfer for schooling
  • 100-Day Employment: Rural employment program

Pakistan

  • BISP (Benazir Income Support Programme): Unconditional cash transfers
  • Waseela-e-Taleem: Education stipends
  • Waseela-e-Sehat: Health insurance
  • Ehsaas Program: Comprehensive social protection umbrella

Sri Lanka

  • Samurdhi: Comprehensive welfare program with cash + development
  • Elderly Allowance: Universal pension for 70+
  • Disability Allowance: Support for persons with disabilities
  • School Meal Program: Nutrition support in schools

Nepal

  • Old Age Allowance: Universal pension from age 70
  • Child Grant: Cash transfer for children under 5
  • Single Women Allowance: Support for widows, divorced women
  • Endangered Indigenous Peoples Support: Targeted assistance

Digital Innovations in South Asian Safety Nets

JAM Trinity (India)

Components:

  • Jan Dhan: Bank account for every household
  • Aadhaar: Biometric digital identity
  • Mobile: Mobile phone connectivity

Impact: Enables Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) reducing leakages

DBT Savings: ₹ crore saved through reduced leakages

Digital Delivery Innovations

  • One Nation One Ration Card: PDS portability across states
  • PMFBY App: Crop insurance through mobile technology
  • eShram Portal: National database of informal workers
  • Common Service Centers: Digital service delivery in rural areas

Key Challenges in South Asian Safety Nets

Implementation Challenges

  • Leakages: Corruption, ghost beneficiaries, quantity manipulation
  • Targeting errors: Exclusion of deserving, inclusion of non-poor
  • Administrative capacity: Weak delivery systems at grassroots
  • Coordination: Multiple programs with different eligibility criteria

Coverage Gaps

  • Urban poor: Many programs focus on rural areas
  • Migrant workers: Difficulty accessing benefits across states
  • Informal workers: Lack of documentation for formal programs
  • Women's access: Male-headed household bias in targeting

Digital Exclusion

  • Technology access: Limited smartphones, internet connectivity
  • Digital literacy: Inability to navigate digital systems
  • Language barriers: Apps often not in local languages
  • Aadhaar issues: Authentication failures, biometric problems

Social Exclusion in Safety Nets

Marginalized Groups & Access Barriers

Dalit Communities

  • Discrimination in program implementation
  • Separate queues, inferior quality rations
  • Exclusion from leadership roles in implementation

Adivasi/Tribal Communities

  • Geographic isolation from service delivery points
  • Language barriers in documentation
  • Lack of required identity documents

Religious Minorities

  • Lower participation rates in some regions
  • Discrimination in access to credit, employment programs
  • Language and cultural barriers

Women

  • Male control over bank accounts and documents
  • Limited mobility to access services
  • Unpaid care work limiting participation in employment programs

  • Poverty reduction: Impact on multidimensional poverty index

  • Economic empowerment: Women's agency, asset creation
  • Social inclusion: Participation rates across caste, religious groups

Policy Analysis Framework

Evaluating South Asian Safety Net Programs:

1. Design Assessment

  • Is the program addressing the right problem?
  • Are eligibility criteria appropriate for context?
  • Does benefit level meet basic needs?

2. Implementation Analysis

  • How effective is the delivery mechanism?
  • What are inclusion/exclusion error rates?
  • How much leakage occurs?

3. Social Impact

  • Does it reach marginalized communities?
  • How does it affect women's empowerment?
  • What are the social cohesion impacts?

4. Political Economy

  • What are the political incentives for implementation?
  • How sustainable is funding?
  • What are elite capture risks?
