Climate vs Weather: Getting the Basics Right

üî¨ Essential Distinction

Weather: Short-term atmospheric conditions (hours to weeks) in a specific location

  • Example: "Mumbai received 200mm rainfall yesterday"
  • Highly variable and difficult to predict beyond 2 weeks

Climate: Long-term average weather patterns (30+ years) across regions

  • Example: "Mumbai's monsoon season typically brings 2,000mm annual rainfall"
  • Predictable patterns that enable agriculture and urban planning

Climate Change: Long-term shifts in climate patterns due to human activities

  • Changes in temperature, precipitation, extreme events over decades
  • Driven primarily by greenhouse gas emissions since 1750

Part 2: The Greenhouse Effect - Natural vs Enhanced

20 minutes

Understanding Earth's Energy Balance

üåç The Natural Greenhouse Effect

Essential for Life: Without greenhouse gases, Earth's average temperature would be -18¬∞C (currently +15¬∞C)

How It Works:

  1. Solar Energy In: Sun sends shortwave radiation to Earth
  2. Surface Absorption: Earth's surface absorbs energy and warms up
  3. Longwave Radiation Out: Warm surface emits longwave (infrared) radiation
  4. Greenhouse Trapping: Greenhouse gases absorb and re-emit some radiation back to surface
  5. Natural Balance: Keeps Earth warm enough for liquid water and life

The Enhanced Greenhouse Effect: Human activities add extra greenhouse gases, trapping more heat and disrupting Earth's energy balance. This is what drives climate change.

Part 3: The IPCC Framework - Assessing Climate Science

20 minutes

What is the IPCC?

üèõÔ∏è Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change

Established: 1988 by UNEP and WMO

Purpose: Assess scientific literature on climate change for policymakers

Structure: 3 Working Groups + Task Force on National Greenhouse Gas Inventories

  • Working Group I: Physical science basis of climate change
  • Working Group II: Impacts, adaptation, and vulnerability
  • Working Group III: Mitigation of climate change

Process: Thousands of scientists review and synthesize research every 6-8 years

Indian Participation: 50+ Indian scientists contributed to AR6 (2021-2023)

Understanding IPCC Language & Confidence Levels

IPCC Confidence Framework: The IPCC uses specific language to communicate certainty levels based on evidence quality and scientific agreement.

Confidence Levels
  • Very High: At least 9 out of 10 chance of being correct
  • High: About 8 out of 10 chance
  • Medium: About 5 out of 10 chance
  • Low: About 2 out of 10 chance
Likelihood Terms
  • Virtually Certain: 99-100% probability
  • Very Likely: 90-100% probability
  • Likely: 66-100% probability
  • Unlikely: 0-33% probability

Part 4: Climate Scenarios & Future Projections

15 minutes

Understanding Representative Concentration Pathways (RCPs)

üìà Climate Scenarios for South Asia

RCP2.6 (Strong Climate Action):

  • Global warming limited to 1.5-2¬∞C by 2100
  • South Asia: 1.5-2.5¬∞C warming, minimal monsoon change
  • Requires immediate, massive emissions cuts

RCP4.5 (Moderate Climate Action):

  • Global warming of 2.5-3¬∞C by 2100
  • South Asia: 2-3.5¬∞C warming, 10-15% monsoon increase
  • Current national pledges lead roughly here

RCP8.5 (High Emissions/Business as Usual):

  • Global warming of 4-5¬∞C by 2100
  • South Asia: 4-6¬∞C warming, 20-30% monsoon increase
  • Continued fossil fuel dependence

üáÆüá≥ India-Specific Projections (IPCC AR6)

Temperature Changes by 2050:

  • All scenarios: 1.5-2.5¬∞C warming above 1995-2014 baseline
  • Heat waves will be longer, more intense, and more frequent
  • Himalayan regions warming faster than global average

Precipitation Changes:

  • Annual rainfall: +5% to +15% increase
  • Monsoon intensity: More extreme rainfall events
  • Regional variation: Northeast gets wetter, northwest drier

Extreme Events:

  • Heat waves: 3-5x more frequent by mid-century
  • Flooding: Increased urban and coastal flood risk
  • Cyclones: More intense storms in Arabian Sea
  • Droughts: More frequent in central/western regions
